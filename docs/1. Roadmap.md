Project Title: **Sentimental-Behavioral Debt Recovery (SBDR): A Multi-Modal AI Framework for Compassionate Collections**

1. Executive Summary & Problem Statement

Traditional financial collections systems are **binary and reactive**, focusing on "recovery at any cost" once a default has occurred. This leads to customer churn, high legal costs, and negative social impact.  
**The Goal:** Build a **proactive, multi-modal system** that identifies "Financial Distress" before a total default happens by merging **transactional sequences** with **communication sentiment**, recommending personalized, compassionate interventions (e.g., restructured plans vs. simple reminders).

---

2. Project Aim & Objectives

- **Primary Aim:** Move from a "Will they pay?" classification to an "Intervention Optimization" recommendation engine.
- **Objectives:**
    1. Detect "Life Event" anomalies in spending sequences using **Deep Learning**.
    2. Quantify "Financial Anxiety" from customer communications using **NLP**.
    3. Classify customers into "Persona-based Recovery Tiers" (e.g., Temporary Hardship, Habitual Delinquency, Fraudulent Intent).
    4. Implement **Explainable AI (XAI)** to ensure the "Compassionate" suggestion is auditable.
    5. Detect **Strategic Default** behavior by isolating customers who demonstrate deliberate non-payment despite financial capacity, using a dedicated rule-based **Audit Layer (B3.5)** to ensure legally defensible escalation separate from genuine hardship cases.

4. Methodology & Architecture

Phase A: Data Preparation (The "Hybrid" Approach)

- **Structured Data:** Use the UCI Credit Card Default Dataset for core financial metrics.
- **Unstructured Data:** Generate/use synthetic customer support tickets (e.g., using GPT-4o) that vary in tone (Anxious, Angry, Cooperative, Avoidant).
- **Feature Engineering:** Calculate "Volatility of Spending" (Standard Deviation of monthly spend) and "Sentiment Shift" (Change in tone over the last 3 messages).

Phase B: The Multi-Modal Pipeline

1. **Branch 1 (NLP):** FinBERT processes chat logs to output a "Distress Score" (0.0 to 1.0).
2. **Branch 2 (DL):** An LSTM processes the last 6 months of payment/spend history to detect "Hidden Stress" patterns.
3. **Branch 3 (ML):** A Gradient Boosting model takes the outputs of Branch 1 & 2 + Static Demographic data.
4. **Branch 3.5 (Audit Layer):** A rule-based Strategic Default filter that runs *after* XGBoost classification. Triggered only for Tier 5 predictions. Cross-checks five hard thresholds — income stability, pre-default spending surge (Bust-Out pattern), selective delinquency, communication absence, and elevated fraud rate — before any legal escalation is confirmed. Rule-based by design to ensure full auditability and legal defensibility. If any threshold fails, the customer is downgraded to Tier 4 for human review.

Phase C: Output & Intervention

Instead of a "Yes/No" default prediction, the model outputs one of five **Action Tiers**:

- **Tier 1: Standard Reminder** (Low risk, neutral sentiment).
- **Tier 2: Soft Assistance** (High volatility, neutral sentiment) -> Offer "Skip a Payment."
- **Tier 3: Hardship Restructuring** (High distress sentiment, declining spend) -> Offer a 6-month plan.
- **Tier 4: Legal/Recovery** (Zero communication, total payment stop).
- **Tier 5: Strategic Default / Fraud Escalation** (Stable income + bust-out spending + deliberate communication silence + selective delinquency across account types) → Confirmed via B3.5 Audit Layer → Legal and fraud team escalation. Conservative threshold: only confirmed when all five audit conditions are met. When in doubt, classify as Tier 4 and escalate to human review.

## 7. Risk Register — Strategic Default Detection (Tier 5)

| Risk | Description | Mitigation |
|------|-------------|------------|
| **Class Imbalance** | Tier 5 represents ~5–10% of customers, causing XGBoost to underpredict this class | Apply SMOTE oversampling + set XGBoost class_weight to penalize missed Tier 5 predictions |
| **False Positive — Ethical** | Wrongly flagging genuine distress as strategic default leads to legal escalation against a suffering customer | Conservative threshold — default to Tier 4 when uncertain. Only escalate to Tier 5 when all five audit conditions are met simultaneously |
| **Feature Leakage** | Using post-default information in training causes model to fail in production | Feature timestamp audit — enforce that all features reflect information available before or at the time of the first missed payment |
| **Demographic Bias** | Tier 5 flags may disproportionately impact customers of certain age, gender, or education level | Post-training fairness audit specifically on Tier 5 predictions. If SEX, AGE, or EDUCATION are top SHAP drivers for Tier 5, treat as a red flag requiring model revision |
| **Audit Explainability** | Fraud accusations must be legally defensible — model explanation alone is insufficient | Every B3.5 rule documented with explicit business justification. SHAP output must specify which exact signals triggered Tier 5 |
| **Synthetic Data Limitation** | Tier 5 training examples are synthetically constructed, not real observed fraud cases | Acknowledge in paper as a deployment caveat. In production, model requires retraining on confirmed historical fraud cases |
