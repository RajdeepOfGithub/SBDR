We will use a **Hybrid Data Strategy**. Since real bank chat logs are private, we will merge a high-quality **loan dataset** with a **financial sentiment dataset** to simulate the link between money and behavior.

1. Core Financial Datasets (The "Behavior")

These provide the transaction history, payment status, and customer profiles you need for your ML and Deep Learning models. 

- **Lending Club Loan Data (Kaggle):** This is the "gold standard" for credit risk. It includes over **2 million rows** of loan status (Current, Late, Fully Paid), borrower income, and credit scores.
- **[Default of Credit Card Clients Dataset](https://archive.ics.uci.edu/ml/datasets/default+of+credit+card+clients) (UCI Repository):** A cleaner, smaller dataset (30,000 records) that tracks 6 months of payment history and bill statements. It is excellent for training **LSTMs** to detect declining payment patterns.
- **[Sparkov Synthetic Financial Transactions](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/kartik2112/fraud-detection) (Kaggle):** Contains millions of simulated transactions. You can use this to create "daily spending profiles" for your users. 

2. NLP & Sentiment Datasets (The "Vibe")

You will use these to train your NLP model (Branch 1) to recognize signs of financial stress in text.

- **Financial PhraseBank (Hugging Face):** Contains thousands of sentences from financial news and reports labeled by sentiment. It is the best starting point for fine-tuning a **FinBERT** model.
- **[Sentiment Analysis for Mental Health](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/suchintikasarkar/sentiment-analysis-for-mental-health) (Kaggle):** Useful for training your model to recognize "Anxiety" or "Stress" keywords that might appear in customer support chats.
- **Reddit Financial Sentiment Dataset (ScienceDirect):** Data from subreddits like r/personalfinance where people discuss real debt struggles. You can scrape similar data or use this dataset to understand how "distress" is voiced. 

3. How to Merge Them (The "Secret Sauce")

To make this a unique capstone, you must **join** these datasets using a common key.

1. **Select a Customer ID** from your Lending Club or UCI dataset.
2. **Assign a Sentiment Category:** If the customer is "Late" in the financial data, map a "High Stress" text sample from your NLP dataset to that specific ID.
3. **Result:** You now have a **Multi-Modal Dataset** where a single "Sarah" has both a declining bank balance (Numbers) and an anxious chat log (Text).
